# BetterDose Public Website Overview

> This document outlines exactly what an external reviewer (such as a compliance agent, bank, payment processor, or potential client) will see when reviewing the public-facing BetterDose website. 
> 
> *Note: External reviewers do not see the internal `/admin` dashboard or any underlying code structure.*

---

## What the Reviewer Sees

When a reviewer visits `betterdose.website` (or your live domain), they see a highly professional, actively operating software development business. Every page is designed to build trust and prove that real software engineering work is being delivered to clients.

### 1. The Home Page (`/`)
- **First Impression:** A "Available for development contracts" badge immediately signals this is an active business seeking clients.
- **Business Identity:** It explicitly states the business is registered in the **United Kingdom** with the engineering team operating from **Addis Ababa, Ethiopia**.
- **Service Summary:** A clean grid outlining the core services offered (Web Apps, APIs, Automation).
- **Recent Work:** The 3 most recent projects are displayed immediately on the homepage to prove competency.
- **Payment Terms:** A short, professional note mentioning that international payments are processed via formal invoices to Wise or PayPal.

### 2. The Portfolio (`/portfolio`)
- **Categorized Work:** Projects are broken down into **Completed**, **Ongoing**, and **University** (academic) projects.
- **Proof of Work:** Reviewers can click into individual projects to see:
  - Technical architecture and the exact tech stack used.
  - Links to the **Live Demo** of the project.
  - Links to the **GitHub Repository** showing the actual source code.
  - *This is the strongest proof for any compliance reviewer that the business is real and the software is actually built by the team.*

### 3. Business Activity Timeline (`/activity`)
- **Active Operations:** Shows a timeline of recent software builds, feature shipments, and deployed applications.
- **GitHub Commits:** Displays recent code commit activity directly from the team's GitHub accounts (Nebiyu, Eyob, Abel), proving that code is being written *right now*.

### 4. Client Deliverables (`/deliverables`)
- **Value Exchange:** Clearly explains what clients are paying for (Source Code, REST APIs, Automation Scripts, Database Schemas, Deployment).
- This answers the compliance question: *"What is the good or service being sold?"*

### 5. The Operating Team (`/about`)
- **Real People:** Profiles for the three engineers: Nebiyu Muluadam, Eyob Mulugeta, and Abel Tadesse.
- **Academic Credibility:** Displays their `aau.edu.et` (Addis Ababa University) email addresses, proving they are legitimate engineering students/professionals.
- **Technical Skills:** Lists the specific programming languages and frameworks each developer specializes in.

### 6. Official Business Information (`/business-info`)
- **Corporate Registry:** Displays the official UK registration country.
- **Operational Location:** Lists the physical operating base in Addis Ababa, Ethiopia.
- **Contact Channels:** Official business emails and contact details.
- This page is built specifically for banks and payment processors to easily verify business identity.

### 7. Code Integrity (`/github-profiles`)
- Direct links to the public GitHub profiles of the development team.
- Highlights verifiable repositories, proving the team has the technical skills they are selling.

## Why this structure passes compliance reviews

Payment processors (like Wise, Stripe, or PayPal) review websites looking for **authenticity**, **transparency**, and a **clear exchange of value**. 

This site passes those reviews because:
1. **It hides nothing:** The team's names, academic backgrounds, and physical locations are public.
2. **It proves the work:** Instead of fake marketing metrics, the site links directly to GitHub repositories and live deployments.
3. **It explains the service clearly:** The deliverables page removes any ambiguity about what clients receive in exchange for their money.
4. **It looks active:** The activity timeline and ongoing projects show that the business is currently operating, not abandoned.
